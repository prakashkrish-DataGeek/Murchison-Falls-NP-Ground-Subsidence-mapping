Subsidence Metric

ΔVV / σ

Seasonal-baseline-removed Z-score anomaly. Red = strong surface change signal.

📡 Data Source

Google Earth Engine collection COPERNICUS/S1_GRD. Ascending orbit pass filtered to IW mode, VV polarisation. Monthly median composites computed for each calendar month. Exported at 1 km grid via sample(scale=1000).

📐 Anomaly Method

Per-pixel seasonal baseline = mean VV of the same calendar month in the first 12 months (May 2022–Apr 2023). Anomaly dB = VV(t) − baseline_same_month. Z-score = anomaly / per-pixel σ. Intensity = |Z| / 3 clipped to [0, 1].

⚠️ Interpretation Notes

This is a backscatter variance proxy, not true InSAR. High anomaly = surface change (vegetation loss, flooding, ground deformation). For mm-precision vertical displacement use ASF HyP3 SBAS or MintPy time-series InSAR.

🌍 Environmental Context

MFNP sits on Pleistocene lacustrine sediments near Lake Albert, a rift-system lake subject to tectonic activity. The Albert Nile delta and wetland margins are highest-risk zones for sediment compaction and hydraulic subsidence.
